A calmer first day aboard

The Calm Embarkation Checklist

Use this sequence during the week before sailing, arrival morning, and your first 90 minutes aboard. The goal is to finish the time-sensitive tasks without turning embarkation into a race.

Important: Current cruise-line instructions, identification requirements, terminal directions, muster procedures, and crew guidance always take priority over this checklist.

One Week Before Sailing

Confirm terminal, arrival appointment, parking or transportation, and required identification.
Download or update the cruise-line app and sign in before leaving home.
Choose the three onboard experiences your group would most regret missing.
Pick one backup option for each priority in case times or availability change.
Review the ship enough to identify forward, midship, aft, and your cabin deck.
Save cruise documents, confirmations, and travel details for offline access.

Arrival Morning

Verify passports or other required identification are physically in hand.
Check official notifications before heading to the terminal.
Eat, hydrate, and leave enough margin for traffic, luggage, security, and check-in.
Keep medication, valuables, electronics, documents, and first-day essentials in the carry-on.

The First 90 Minutes Aboard

Connect to the ship network, open the app, and confirm the current schedule.
Complete urgent reservations or limited-capacity tasks before casual exploring.
Find the cabin corridor and nearest useful stair or elevator bank.
Eat at a planned alternative instead of automatically joining the first crowded line.
Walk one continuous loop connecting your cabin, dinner area, evening venue, and meeting point.
Confirm dinner, one main evening activity, and a specific fallback meeting location.
