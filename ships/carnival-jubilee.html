Carnival Jubilee at a glance

Jubilee is the most distinctive of Carnival’s current Excel-class ships because its ocean-themed zones give the lower-deck experience a strong identity beyond the familiar Lido and attraction areas.

Bottom line: Jubilee rewards guests who learn Currents, The Shores, Grand Central, and Summer Landing as separate neighborhoods. It becomes tiring when every meal and activity requires a full-ship crossing.

Who should book Carnival Jubilee?

Strong match

  • Families who want WaterWorks, BOLT, sports, kids’ programming, and many food choices.
  • Groups with different interests who need multiple daytime and evening zones.
  • Guests who enjoy large ships, energetic entertainment, and destination-style neighborhoods.
  • Cruisers who want a new-build ship from Galveston.

Think twice

  • You prefer a compact, quiet ship that can be learned in one walk.
  • Long corridors or elevator dependence are major concerns.
  • You dislike reservations or making many daily choices.
  • You expect the central pool deck to feel calm during sea-day peak periods.

Choose four anchors before sailing

  • Breakfast anchor: one default venue when nobody wants to debate.
  • Daytime anchor: Lido, Summer Landing, Serenity, or another agreed zone.
  • Evening anchor: Grand Central, Currents, or The Shores.
  • Fallback meeting point: one named venue everyone can find.

Dining strategy

Jubilee offers enough food variety to avoid buffet dependence, but a clear system prevents overbooking, overspending, and unnecessary ship crossings.

Included quick default

Big Chicken

Reliable for breakfast or casual meals. The simple menu makes it useful for mixed groups.

Included rotating option

Street Eats

Compact stations with changing items. Best for sampling or a quick lunch.

Signature zone

Emeril’s Bistro 717

Creole-influenced food in Currents. Strong choice for a distinctive meal without committing the entire evening to formal dining.

Italian option

Cucina del Capitano

Good family-style choice when the group wants an easy shared meal.

Special occasion

Rudi’s Seagrill

Seafood-focused specialty dining that works best when the meal itself is the evening event.

Small capacity

Bonsai Teppanyaki

Interactive dining with limited seating. Reserve early when it is a must-do.

Aft alternative

Guy’s Pig & Anchor

Barbecue, brewery atmosphere, and live music make Summer Landing useful for both food and evening entertainment.

Casual promenade

Pizza and sandwiches in The Shores

Useful when the group wants a fast meal without returning to the main Lido zone.

  1. Pick two fast defaults. These prevent group indecision.
  2. Choose one paid priority. Reserve the meal you would most regret missing.
  3. Leave one dinner flexible. Use it for a newly discovered venue or schedule recovery.
  4. Eat by zone. Keep dinner near the show, bar, or activity already planned.
Verify current terms: pricing, included meals, repeat-visit rules, menus, and reservations can change.

The first 90 minutes

  1. Connect to the app and confirm schedules.
  2. Handle limited-capacity priorities. BOLT, dining, and must-do reservations come first.
  3. Find the cabin corridor. Learn the nearest useful stair bank and your position on the ship.
  4. Eat away from the first bottleneck.
  5. Walk one zone loop. Connect Grand Central, Currents, The Shores, and Summer Landing.
  6. Set the evening. Confirm dinner, one event, and a meeting point.

Frequently asked questions

Is Carnival Jubilee too big for a first cruise?

Not necessarily. It is a strong first cruise for guests who want variety and will learn the zone layout before boarding.

Do I need to pay extra to enjoy the ship?

No. Many dining, entertainment, and activity options are included. Extra spending mainly expands specialty dining, drinks, BOLT, spa services, and premium retreats.

Which areas feel calmer?

Summer Landing, Serenity, and parts of The Shores can provide a useful contrast to the main Lido environment.

Is a Family Harbor cabin worth it?

It can be when lounge access and family-focused benefits match your routine. It is not automatically worth the premium if the location is less convenient.

How many paid meals should I reserve?

One or two planned specialty meals plus one flexible evening is a strong starting point for many cruisers.
